Projects

Spotify Playlist Utilities

A collection of utilities and experiments for managing Spotify playlists. The main motivation for putting this together was a really obscure and annoying issue with the way music is hosted on Spotify. Playlists that have been edited, listened to, and otherwise enjoyed for several years may accumulate multiple versions of the same track since Spotify is unable to recognize re-uploaded tracks. You might not notice the issue until you've heard the same song three times in a row in a playlist with 750 songs, but I sure have.

TypeScript React Next.js MongoDB REST API

AgroGuardian

An all-terrain vehicle (ATV) crash detection system and companion iOS app. ATV accidents are a leading cause of death in global agribusiness. Moreover, the vast majority of these accidents are all the same type, rollovers. Our research focused on developing a crash detection device that could be mounted on an ATV and alert first-responders in the event of a rollover. The project required out-of-the-box thinking since we had to network prototypes that were operating well outside of cellular networks. Fortunately, we were able to leverage the Iridium satellite network to send crash reports to our cloud infrastructure and alert first-responders.

Python Raspberry Pi AWS PostgreSQL Major Tom

Newsletters

I recently decided to take a social media detox and have become a newsletter guy. It turns out having an actually writing team is a way better way to get news than that random angry guy on Twitter a recommendation algorithm thought you would "engage" with. Here are a few newsletters that I follow to keep an ear to the ground.

  • bioGraphic

    Other than having an amazing name, bioGraphic is home to some of the best environmental reporting. I originally subscribed after they merged with Hakai Magazine. Particularly, I love learning about marine life and conservation efforts that are being undertaken to help preserve the ecosystems we have left. Hakai's weekly newsletter is a wonderful way to start a Friday morning and has given me a much stronger appreciation for our water-bound friends.

  • Axios AM

    Axios AM delivers clear, concise reports each morning to brief you with the day's news in a nutshell. If you get really into it, they have local newsletters too.

  • After School

    Taking a social media detox like me? Want to stay in-the-know on Gen Z trends and the chaos around them? Casey Lewis has you covered. This newsletter is a fun one and amasses a bunch of articles written about Gen Z with some sassy summaries if you are just surfing headlines.

College Radio

One more thing if you'll indulge me. I love music, you (hopefully) love music, and a lot of college students love music. So, why not support your local college radio station? Here are a few of my favorites (totally unbiased):

  • KDVS

    Like I said, I'm totally not biased. I totally did not work down the hall from these folks in the Freeborn Hall basement for two years. KDVS is the University of California, Davis' radio station. You should definitely check them out, even if it's only to oogle at their outrageously large vinyl collection.

  • WMBR

    WMBR is the Massachusetts Institute of Technology's gem of a station and it's the first on your FM dial if you're in the Boston area so you don't even have to remember a number. If you want something truly unique, you have to tune in to Backwoods with the legendary John Funke. I know of no better way to make a trip to the Somerville Market Basket even more surreal than listening to John ramble on over the sounds of birds chirping, construction constructing, the occasional ice cream truck jingle jingling, and, of course, the very best in songs about Cadillacs and chickens (yes, really).

  • WREK

    Also not biased. WREK is coming at us live from the Georgia Institute of Technology. Not only named after a certain ramblin' 1930s Ford Model A, this station is also one of the top 10 highest-powered college radio stations and broadcasts at 100,000 watts. Besides having a great selection of ambient and electronic music WREK is also home to an extremely talented design team. I mean, just look at their website and merch!
