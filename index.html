Projects

Spotify Playlist Utilities

A collection of utilities and experiments for managing Spotify playlists. The main motivation for putting this together was a really obscure and annoying issue with the way music is hosted on Spotify. Playlists that have been edited, listened to, and otherwise enjoyed for several years may accumulate multiple versions of the same track since Spotify is unable to recognize re-uploaded tracks. You might not notice the issue until you've heard the same song three times in a row in a playlist with 750 songs, but I sure have.

TypeScript React Next.js MongoDB REST API

AgroGuardian

An all-terrain vehicle (ATV) crash detection system and companion iOS app. ATV accidents are a leading cause of death in global agribusiness. Moreover, the vast majority of these accidents are all the same type, rollovers. Our research focused on developing a crash detection device that could be mounted on an ATV and alert first-responders in the event of a rollover. The project required out-of-the-box thinking since we had to network prototypes that were operating well outside of cellular networks. Fortunately, we were able to leverage the Iridium satellite network to send crash reports to our cloud infrastructure and alert first-responders.

Newsletters

I recently decided to take a social media detox and have become a newsletter guy. I have come to love the level of care and curation that goes into high-quality publications. They certainly beat that one guy rambling on Reddit. Here are a few newsletters that I follow to keep an ear to the ground.

  • Hakai Magazine

    I love learning about marine life and conservation efforts that are being undertaken to help preserve the ecosystems we have left. Hakai's weekly newsletter is a wonderful way to start a Friday morning and has given me a much stronger appreciation for our water-bound friends.

  • Developer Purpose

    I found Bennett Garner's newsletter through one of his YouTube videos that popped up on my feed. I was impressed with his commitment to filtering out the noise many engineers face when trying to find meaning in their careers. In an ecosystem where echo chambers form around chasing TC and FAANG, it's easy to get worn out. Bennett's newsletter is a breath of fresh air that gets my head back into the game and gives me some big questions to kick around as I navigate my life and career.

  • Axios AM

    Could I work in tech without recommending Axios? Probably, but this media outlet is overflowing with the same energy that drives Silicon Valley's startup culture. Axios AM delivers clear, concise reports each morning to brief you with the day's news in a nutshell. If you get really into it, they have local newsletters too.

  • After School

    Taking a social media detox like me? Want to stay in-the-know on Gen Z trends and the chaos around them? Casey Lewis has you covered. This newsletter is a fun one and amasses a bunch of articles written about Gen Z with some sassy summaries if you are just surfing headlines.

  • Fast Forward

    This weekly newsletter is pretty new to me. It's AI-centric, but has a smattering of additional tech articles featured throughout. I think a lot of the stuff offers solid tech journalism without getting into the weeds.
